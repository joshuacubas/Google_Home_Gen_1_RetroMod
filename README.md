# Google_Home_Gen_1_RetroMod
Retro Modded Gen 1 Google Home Speaker. Installed into vintage 80's Sony Radio. 

# Synopsis:

Dissassemble a Gen 1 Google home speaker. Strip down to the logicboard and install into an 80's Sony wooden speaker. There are many videos only of retromodding in this fashion. But I haven't seen one that adds the ability to keep the speakers capacitive touch features.

The wood on the speaker I chose is fairly thin. My idea was initially to touch the top left and right corners and have the touch register to the Mini. I planned to add copper plates to serve as the receptors and wire to te original contact points on the logic board. I concluded too thin copper and I would not get enough reception of finger touch pickup. Likewise, too thick and it would bee way too receptive. So I guessed and ordered 99.9% a Pure Copper Sheet, 6" x 6", 20 Gauge(0.8mm) Thickness. In my experiment, this was too thick, but not unusable. But the pick up is so strong I can receive "touch" input  with just a hover before ever physically making contact from finger tip to wood. In hindsight I would have used a thinner gauge. But this experiment was a proof of concept, idea to reality.

# Tips:

0 - I used this teardown guide here from ifixit.com; https://www.ifixit.com/Teardown/Google+Home+Mini+Teardown/102264

1 - Buy a torx t6 driver or bit, much easier and safer than hacking or cutting through the case as others on YouTube have shown.

2 - Leave the speaker wire connected to the board. The connector's respective pins on the board are very fragile, not worth breaking, as the board may be come unuseable. Instead cut the wire at the speaker end, strip the plastic sheath and solder to longer wires connecting to the speaker terminals of the Radio or cabinet you are modifying.

https://guide-images.cdn.ifixit.com/igi/c3wNmPscxL5qiBRM.full
![Speaker Wire (Cut on left end in Picture)](https://guide-images.cdn.ifixit.com/igi/c3wNmPscxL5qiBRM.full)

3 - I chose Gen 1 because it uses capacitive touch receptors that are easy to extend and modify, other versions use microfones and kind of use sound mappings to determine finger tap locations (as far I remeber understanding).

4 - Mounting a micro-usb extension on the outside of the box was a smart after thought I later implemented. This eliminates cable stress, and allows you to unplug cords.

5 - Future thoughts, I want to use optical cables to extend the light from the leds my next attempt, different copper thickness, maybe a way to keep original radio functional as well, and figure out how to extend mute and reset physical buttons.
